I'm an Assistant Professor in condensed matter physics at Michigan State University (MSU), and a member of the MSU Theory Center for Non-equilibrium Phenomena.

I work at the interface of condensed matter physics and quantum information science, with a particular interest in non-equilibrium quantum systems. My research focuses on how quantum mechanics gives rise to surprising collective phenomena at macroscopic scales, and on finding ways to harness these effects in new technologies.

Before joining MSU, I previously worked with the guidance of Norman Yao at Harvard, Liang Fu at MIT, Anushya Chandran at Boston University, and Andrew Green at University College London.
